Oolong Tea

Oolong Tea 乌龙茶

The perfect balance between green and black tea

About Oolong Tea

Oolong Tea (乌龙茶) is a traditional Chinese tea that falls between green and black tea in terms of oxidation. It is partially oxidized, giving it a unique flavor profile that combines the freshness of green tea with the complexity of black tea. The name "oolong" means "black dragon" in Chinese, referring to the dark, curled appearance of the tea leaves.

Famous Varieties

  • Tieguanyin (铁观音) - Iron Goddess of Mercy
  • Da Hong Pao (大红袍) - Big Red Robe
  • Dong Ding (冻顶) - Frozen Summit
  • Phoenix Dan Cong (凤凰单枞) - Phoenix Single Bush
  • Milk Oolong (奶香乌龙) - Creamy variety

Brewing Process

  1. Heat water to 85-95°C (185-203°F)
  2. Rinse tea leaves with hot water
  3. Add 3-5 grams of tea leaves per cup
  4. Pour hot water over leaves
  5. Steep for 1-3 minutes
  6. Enjoy multiple infusions (3-7 times)

Cultural Significance

Oolong tea has a rich cultural heritage in China, particularly in Fujian and Guangdong provinces. It is often associated with the Gongfu tea ceremony, a traditional way of preparing and serving tea that emphasizes the appreciation of tea's aroma, taste, and appearance. Oolong tea is also valued for its ability to change flavor with each infusion, creating a dynamic tasting experience.

Where to Experience

Some of the best places to experience authentic oolong tea culture include:

  • Wuyi Mountains - Home of Da Hong Pao
  • Anxi County - Birthplace of Tieguanyin
  • Taiwan's Alishan Mountains - Famous for high-mountain oolongs
  • Traditional tea houses in Fujian and Guangdong

Serving and Appreciation

Oolong tea is traditionally served in small cups using the Gongfu tea ceremony method. This involves multiple short infusions to fully appreciate the tea's evolving flavors. The ceremony emphasizes the visual appreciation of the tea leaves, the aroma of the tea, and the taste of each infusion, creating a complete sensory experience.
