Dragon Boat Festival (端午节)

The Festival of Dragon Boats and Zongzi

← Back to Culture

Overview

The Dragon Boat Festival, also known as Duanwu Festival, is celebrated on the 5th day of the 5th lunar month. It commemorates the patriotic poet Qu Yuan and features dragon boat races, eating zongzi (sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves), and various customs to ward off evil spirits.

History and Significance

The festival originated over 2,000 years ago during the Warring States period. It commemorates Qu Yuan, a loyal minister who drowned himself in the Miluo River after being exiled by the king. Local people raced their boats to save him and threw rice dumplings into the water to prevent fish from eating his body.

Traditional Customs

Dragon boat racing is the most iconic activity of the festival. Teams paddle in long, narrow boats decorated with dragon heads and tails. Other customs include hanging pouches of herbs, wearing colorful silk threads, and displaying images of Zhong Kui (a mythical figure who drives away evil spirits).

Traditional Foods

Zongzi (粽子) is the traditional food of the Dragon Boat Festival. These pyramid-shaped dumplings are made of glutinous rice wrapped in bamboo leaves and filled with various ingredients like meat, beans, or nuts. They are steamed or boiled and enjoyed by families during the festival.

Modern Celebrations

Today, the Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated with international dragon boat racing competitions, cultural performances, and food festivals. Many cities host large-scale events with multiple races, music performances, and food stalls. The festival has also gained popularity outside China, with dragon boat races held in many countries.

Travel Tips

If you're visiting China during the Dragon Boat Festival (usually in June), head to cities with strong dragon boat racing traditions like Hong Kong, Guangzhou, or Suzhou. The races are most exciting in the morning. Many riverside parks and venues host special events and performances. Don't miss trying different varieties of zongzi from local vendors.
