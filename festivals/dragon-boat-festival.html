About Dragon Boat Festival

The Dragon Boat Festival, also known as Duanwu Festival, is celebrated on the 5th day of the 5th lunar month. It commemorates the patriotic poet Qu Yuan, who drowned himself in the Miluo River in 278 BCE. The festival is marked by dragon boat races, eating zongzi (sticky rice dumplings), and hanging aromatic pouches to ward off evil spirits.

History and Significance

The Dragon Boat Festival has a history of over 2,300 years. It originated from the commemoration of Qu Yuan, a patriotic poet and statesman of the Chu Kingdom during the Warring States period. After being exiled and learning of his kingdom's defeat, Qu Yuan drowned himself in the Miluo River. Local people raced their boats to search for his body and threw rice dumplings into the water to prevent fish from eating his remains.

278 BCE

Qu Yuan drowned himself in the Miluo River after learning of his kingdom's defeat.

Han Dynasty (206 BCE - 220 CE)

The festival became an official holiday with dragon boat races and zongzi eating.

Tang Dynasty (618 - 907)

The festival gained popularity with royal celebrations and poetry competitions.

Modern Era

The festival is celebrated worldwide with dragon boat races, cultural performances, and traditional foods.

Traditional Customs

The Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated with various customs and activities. Dragon boat races are the most exciting event, with teams paddling to the beat of drums. People eat zongzi (sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves), hang aromatic pouches, and wear colorful silk threads to ward off evil spirits. Some also drink realgar wine and take herbal baths for health benefits.

Dragon Boat Races

Teams compete in colorful dragon-shaped boats, paddling to the rhythm of drums. The races symbolize the search for Qu Yuan's body.

Zongzi Making

Families gather to make zongzi, sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves with various fillings like meat, beans, or dates.

Aromatic Pouches

Colorful pouches filled with aromatic herbs are hung around the neck or in homes to ward off evil spirits and bring good health.

Herbal Baths

Taking baths with herbs like mugwort and calamus is believed to protect against diseases and evil spirits.

Traditional Foods

The most iconic food of the Dragon Boat Festival is zongzi (粽子), sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves. These pyramid-shaped dumplings come in various flavors, including savory options with pork, chicken, or salted egg yolk, and sweet versions with red bean paste or dates. Other traditional foods include realgar wine and seasonal fruits.

Zongzi (粽子)

Sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves, available in savory flavors (pork, chicken) and sweet varieties (red bean, dates).

Realgar Wine

Traditional wine mixed with realgar (a mineral) is consumed in small amounts to ward off evil spirits and insects.

Seasonal Fruits

Fresh seasonal fruits like lychees, longans, and plums are enjoyed during the festival for their cooling properties.

Modern Celebrations

Today's Dragon Boat Festival celebrations combine traditional customs with modern sports and entertainment. International dragon boat racing competitions attract teams from around the world. Cities host cultural festivals featuring traditional performances, food stalls, and craft demonstrations. The festival has also become a popular tourist attraction, with many people traveling to experience the races and cultural activities.

Travel Tips

If you're visiting China during the Dragon Boat Festival, you'll find celebrations in most cities, with the largest events in major metropolitan areas. The best places to experience the festival include Hong Kong, Guangzhou, and Suzhou, which are known for their spectacular dragon boat races. Book accommodations early as hotels fill up quickly during this period.

Best Locations

Visit cities like Hong Kong, Guangzhou, and Suzhou for the most spectacular dragon boat races and celebrations.

Planning

Book accommodations and transportation well in advance. The festival attracts large crowds, especially for the races.

Experiences

Don't forget your camera! The dragon boat races and colorful celebrations create stunning photo opportunities.
