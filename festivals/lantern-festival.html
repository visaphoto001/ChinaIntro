Lantern Festival (元宵节)

The Festival of Lights and Riddles

← Back to Culture

Overview

The Lantern Festival, also known as Yuanxiao Festival, is celebrated on the 15th day of the first lunar month, marking the final day of the Chinese New Year celebrations. It is a time for lighting lanterns, solving riddles, and eating sweet rice balls called tangyuan.

History and Significance

The Lantern Festival has a history of over 2,000 years. It originated during the Han Dynasty when Emperor Ming ordered lanterns to be lit in temples to show respect to Buddha. Over time, it evolved into a popular celebration where people light lanterns to guide spirits and pray for good fortune in the coming year.

Traditional Customs

During the Lantern Festival, people hang colorful lanterns outside their homes and in public spaces. Children carry small lanterns while walking the streets. Riddle-solving is a popular activity, with riddles written on lanterns for people to solve. The festival also includes dragon and lion dances, and performances of traditional Chinese opera.

Traditional Foods

The most iconic food of the Lantern Festival is tangyuan (汤圆), sweet glutinous rice balls filled with sesame paste, red bean paste, or other sweet fillings. These round dumplings symbolize family unity and completeness. They are typically served in a sweet soup.

Modern Celebrations

Today, the Lantern Festival is celebrated with large-scale lantern displays in parks and public spaces. Cities host lantern fairs with elaborate light shows, cultural performances, and food stalls. Many people also release sky lanterns or floating lanterns on water as a symbol of letting go of the past year's troubles.

Travel Tips

If you're visiting China during the Lantern Festival, head to major cities like Beijing, Shanghai, or Xi'an for spectacular lantern displays. The festival usually falls in February or early March. Be prepared for large crowds, especially in the evening when the lanterns are lit. Many temples and parks host special events and performances.
