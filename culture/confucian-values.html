Core Principles

Confucian values have shaped Chinese society for over two millennia, emphasizing moral cultivation, social harmony, and proper relationships between individuals.

Five Constants

The fundamental virtues that guide personal conduct and social relationships.

  • Benevolence (仁 - rén)
  • Righteousness (义 - yì)
  • Propriety (礼 - lǐ)
  • Wisdom (智 - zhì)
  • Trustworthiness (信 - xìn)

Social Relationships

The Five Cardinal Relationships that form the basis of social order.

  • Ruler and Subject
  • Father and Son
  • Husband and Wife
  • Elder and Younger
  • Friend and Friend

Moral Education

The importance of continuous moral cultivation and learning.

  • Self-Cultivation
  • Lifelong Learning
  • Moral Character
  • Leading by Example
"己所不欲，勿施于人" (Do not do to others what you do not want done to yourself)
- Confucius
