Shanghai - The Bund 上海外滩

Where East meets West in architectural splendor

1.5km Length of the Bund
52 Historic Buildings
1844 Year Established

Historical Significance

Colonial Heritage

The Bund's architecture reflects Shanghai's history as a major trading port and international settlement, featuring buildings from various European styles.

Modern Transformation

Today's Bund represents the perfect blend of historical preservation and modern development, symbolizing Shanghai's rise as a global financial center.

Cultural Hub

The area serves as a cultural crossroads, hosting museums, art galleries, and performance venues that showcase both local and international arts.

Must-See Attractions

Peace Hotel (和平饭店)

An iconic Art Deco building that has hosted numerous historical figures and continues to be a symbol of Shanghai's glamorous past.

Customs House (海关大楼)

Famous for its clock tower, this building represents the architectural grandeur of the early 20th century in Shanghai.

Pudong Skyline (浦东天际线)

The view across the Huangpu River offers a stunning contrast between historic architecture and modern skyscrapers.
