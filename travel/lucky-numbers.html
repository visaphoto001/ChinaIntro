Lucky Numbers in Chinese Culture

Understanding the significance of numbers in Chinese culture and their impact on daily life

Most Auspicious Numbers

Numbers in Chinese culture carry deep symbolic meanings and superstitions that influence daily life, business decisions, and social interactions.

8 (八, bā)

The luckiest number in Chinese culture

Associated with wealth, prosperity, and success

Sounds similar to "发" (fā) meaning "to prosper"

6 (六, liù)

Symbolizes smoothness and good luck

Sounds like "溜" (liū) meaning "smooth"

Popular in business and personal life

9 (九, jiǔ)

Represents longevity and eternity

Sounds like "久" (jiǔ) meaning "long-lasting"

Often used in imperial contexts

Numbers to Avoid

Number Pronunciation Reason Impact
4 (四, sì) sì Sounds like "死" (sǐ) meaning "death" Avoided in buildings, phone numbers, license plates
7 (七, qī) qī Sometimes associated with "气" (qì) meaning "anger" Less preferred in some contexts

Common Applications

  • Phone numbers and license plates
  • Building and floor numbers
  • Wedding dates and gift amounts
  • Business names and product pricing
  • Apartment and room numbers
  • Flight numbers and train schedules

Number Combinations

Popular Combinations

  • 888 - Triple prosperity
  • 168 - "一路发" (yī lù fā) - "All the way to prosperity"
  • 518 - "我要发" (wǒ yào fā) - "I want to prosper"
  • 666 - Triple smoothness
  • 999 - Triple longevity

Cultural Significance

  • Numbers influence major life decisions
  • Impact on business and personal relationships
  • Reflect traditional Chinese beliefs and superstitions
  • Show respect for cultural customs
  • Can affect social status and perception

Practical Tips for Visitors

  • When giving gifts, consider lucky number amounts
  • Be aware of floor numbers in buildings (4th floor may be labeled as 3A)
  • Understand that some prices may be set to include lucky numbers
  • Respect local preferences for number choices
  • Learn basic number pronunciation for daily interactions
