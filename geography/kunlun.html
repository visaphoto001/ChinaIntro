About the Kunlun Mountains

The Kunlun Mountains form one of Asia's longest mountain ranges, stretching approximately 3,000 kilometers across western China. These mountains are considered one of the most important ranges in Chinese mythology and geography.

Geological Features

The Kunlun Mountains are part of the larger Tibetan Plateau system and consist of several parallel ranges. The range includes numerous peaks over 6,000 meters, with Kongur Tagh being the highest at 7,649 meters.

Climate and Environment

The range experiences an arid climate with significant temperature variations. The mountains are home to unique alpine ecosystems and serve as a crucial water source for the surrounding regions through their glaciers and snowmelt.

Cultural Significance

In Chinese mythology, the Kunlun Mountains are considered the dwelling place of various deities and immortals. The range has been a source of inspiration for Chinese art, literature, and spiritual practices for thousands of years.
